About

My name is Laure Mari Ho, and I'm driven by a deep curiosity about the natural world and a care for the people in it. I research the genomic basis of evolution, organismal diversity, and gene-environment interactions in the lab of Dr. Peter Sudmant at the University of California, Berkeley, spanning computational genomics, ecological field research, and wet lab methods, with a goal of advancing genetic epidemiology.

Alongside my research, I work in research administration, which has shown me the full picture of what it takes to keep science moving. That same curiosity and care have led me from science to craft (textiles & design, ceramics, and photography, primarily) and sustainable agriculture—and weaving it all together has made me a stronger scientist.

Outside of research, I spend most of my time outdoors—skiing, climbing, and surfing. I grew up in Saratoga, California, caught between the Sierra Nevada and the Pacific, and never lost my pull toward both.
